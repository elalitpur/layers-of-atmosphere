The Five Main Layers of Earth's Atmosphere

1. Troposphere (0–12 km)

The layer we live in. Almost all weather occurs here. Temperature decreases with altitude.

Key Fact: Commercial airplanes fly near the top of the troposphere (≈10–12 km).

2. Stratosphere (12–50 km)

Home to the ozone layer that absorbs most UV radiation. Temperature increases with height.

Key Fact: The ozone layer is located between 15–35 km altitude.

3. Mesosphere (50–85 km)

Meteors usually burn up in this layer. It's the coldest atmospheric layer.

Coldest recorded temperature: –90°C (–130°F)

4. Thermosphere (85–600 km)

Temperature can reach 2,500°C, but it feels cold because molecules are far apart. Auroras occur here.

International Space Station orbits here (~400 km)

5. Exosphere (600–10,000+ km)

The outermost layer that gradually merges with outer space. Satellites orbit here.

Very thin air — atoms can escape into space
